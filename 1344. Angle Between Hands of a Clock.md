# Problem Statement

Given two integers:

```text
hour
minutes
```

representing the time on an analog clock, return the **smaller angle** (in degrees) between the hour hand and the minute hand.

Answers within:

```text
10^-5
```

of the actual answer are accepted.

---

## 🧪 Example 1

```text
Input:
hour = 12
minutes = 30

Output:
165
```

### Explanation

```text
Minute hand:
30 × 6 = 180°

Hour hand:
12:30 = 15°

Difference:
|180 - 15| = 165°
```

---

## 🧪 Example 2

```text
Input:
hour = 3
minutes = 30

Output:
75
```

### Explanation

```text
Minute hand:
30 × 6 = 180°

Hour hand:
3 × 30 + 30 × 0.5
= 105°

Angle:
180 - 105 = 75°
```

---

## 🧪 Example 3

```text
Input:
hour = 3
minutes = 15

Output:
7.5
```

### Explanation

```text
Minute hand:
15 × 6 = 90°

Hour hand:
3 × 30 + 15 × 0.5
= 97.5°

Angle:
97.5 - 90 = 7.5°
```

---

## 📌 Clock Facts

### Minute Hand

```text
360° in 60 minutes

=> 6° per minute
```

Formula:

```text
minute_angle = minutes × 6
```

---

### Hour Hand

```text
360° in 12 hours

=> 30° per hour
```

Also moves continuously:

```text
30° / 60
= 0.5° per minute
```

Formula:

```text
hour_angle =
(hour % 12) × 30
+
minutes × 0.5
```

---

## 💻 Solution (Python)

```python
class Solution:
    def angleClock(self, hour, minutes):

        hour_angle = (hour % 12) * 30 + minutes * 0.5

        minute_angle = minutes * 6

        diff = abs(hour_angle - minute_angle)

        return min(diff, 360 - diff)
```
